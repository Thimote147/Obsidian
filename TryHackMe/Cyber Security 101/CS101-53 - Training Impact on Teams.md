# 🚀 Training Impact on Teams

> [!abstract] Summary
> How continuous training impacts security teams — skill development, measurable performance improvements, retention, and making the business case for security training investment.

**Path:** [[Cyber Security 101]] > Module 13 > Training Impact on Teams

## 📖 Key Concepts

### Why Training Matters
- Cyber has a chronic global skills shortage
- New CVEs and techniques emerge constantly
- Trained analysts detect and respond faster
- Training improves retention — people who grow, stay

### Measuring Training Impact
| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **MTTD** (Mean Time to Detect) | How quickly threats are identified |
| **MTTR** (Mean Time to Respond) | How quickly incidents are contained |
| **False Positive Rate** | Alert quality and analyst efficiency |
| **Phishing Click Rate** | Security awareness effectiveness |

### Training Types
**Technical (hands-on skills):** TryHackMe · HackTheBox · SANS · Immersive Labs

**Security Awareness (all staff):** Phishing simulations · Social engineering · Password hygiene

**Tabletop Exercises:** Simulated incident scenarios — test IR playbooks without real-world pressure

**Purple Teaming:** Red and Blue work together — validates defences against realistic attacks

### Business Case for Training
> Average data breach cost (IBM 2024): ~$4.45M
> Cost of hands-on training platform: <<$1,000/user/year

ROI is clear. The question isn't whether to train — it's how to prioritise.

> [!tip] Track MTTD and MTTR before and after training periods. Showing measurable improvement is the strongest argument for continued training investment.

## 🔑 Key Takeaways
- Training ROI is measurable — MTTD/MTTR both improve with practice
- Hands-on training builds skills that video courses don't
- Security awareness training reduces the human attack surface
- Continuous learning is mandatory — the threat landscape evolves daily
